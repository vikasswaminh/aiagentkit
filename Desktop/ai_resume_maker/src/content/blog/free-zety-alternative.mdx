Zety is one of the most well-known resume builders on the market, and for good reason. It has polished templates, a smooth editor, and content suggestions that help you write each section. But Zety's free tier has a significant limitation: you cannot download your resume without paying.

If you have used Zety, you already know the experience. You spend 20 to 30 minutes entering your work history, choosing a template, and refining your content. Everything looks great in the preview. Then you click "Download" and see a subscription prompt starting at $2.70 per month. The resume you just built is held behind a paywall.

FreeFreeCV is a completely free resume builder with no premium tier. This comparison breaks down exactly what each platform offers, what it costs, and which one delivers more value for job seekers in 2026.

## Zety Pricing Breakdown

Zety operates on a freemium model. The free tier lets you use the editor, browse templates, and preview your resume. The paywall appears at the export step.

**What is free on Zety:**
- Browsing and previewing all templates
- Using the resume editor to enter your information
- Receiving basic content suggestions while writing
- Creating an account and saving your resume

**What requires a paid plan:**
- Downloading your resume as a PDF
- Accessing premium templates
- Using advanced content suggestions and pre-written phrases
- Exporting cover letters
- Removing Zety branding from shared links

**Zety pricing tiers (as of 2026):**
- Monthly plan: $24.99 per month
- Quarterly plan: $10.50 per month (billed as $31.50 every 3 months)
- Annual plan: $2.70 per month (billed as $32.40 per year)

The annual plan brings the per-month cost down, but it requires an upfront annual commitment. Many job seekers only need a resume builder for a few weeks during an active search, making the monthly plan the realistic option — and $24.99 for a single PDF download is a steep price.

Zety also offers a 14-day trial period on some plans, but users have reported that cancellation requires contacting support and that auto-renewal catches many subscribers off guard. Always read the terms carefully before subscribing.
